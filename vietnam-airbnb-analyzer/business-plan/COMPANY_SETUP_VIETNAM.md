# Legal Entity Options for STR (Airbnb) Subleasing in Da Lat, Lam Đồng — 2025/2026

**Scope:** Choosing a company/entity structure for a small operator sub-leasing 1–3 units for short-term rental (STR) in Da Lat. FX used: **26,000 VND/USD**. All "must-confirm-with-a-pro" items are marked **[VERIFY-PRO]**.

> **Note on Da Lat's status (2025 restructuring):** In the mid-2025 administrative reform, three provinces (Lâm Đồng + Bình Thuận + Đắk Nông) merged into a single, enlarged **Lâm Đồng** province, and the district tier was abolished in favor of a **two-tier** (province + commune/ward) model. "Da Lat city" as an administrative unit was dissolved and reorganized into wards (e.g., Xuân Hương–Đà Lạt, Cam Ly, Lâm Viên–Đà Lạt) reporting directly to the province. This changes *which office* you file with (see below). **[VERIFY-PRO]** the exact ward covering your unit's address.

---

## 1. Bottom line up front (BLUF)

The single most decisive fact is **who the owner is**:

- **A foreign individual/company CANNOT own a household business (hộ kinh doanh).** By law that form is reserved for Vietnamese citizens (and naturalized citizens). A foreigner using a Vietnamese "nominee" to hold a household business is a sham arrangement that Vietnamese courts treat as void, and the new Ultimate-Beneficial-Owner (UBO) disclosure rules under **Decree 168/2025/ND-CP** now make it easy to detect.
- **Accommodation/lodging is open to 100% foreign ownership**, but a foreign owner must run it through a company with an **IRC (Investment Registration Certificate) + ERC** — materially more cost, capital, and paperwork.

**Recommendation for 1–3 units (summary; full version in §9):**
- **If you are Vietnamese, or have a genuine, trusted Vietnamese co-owner/spouse:** start lean with a **household business (hộ kinh doanh)** registered by the Vietnamese person, or a **domestic single-member LLC** if you want limited liability, VAT invoices and room to scale. No IRC needed.
- **If you are a foreigner with no real local partner and are committed to doing it cleanly:** a **100% foreign-owned single-member LLC (TNHH MTV)** with IRC+ERC is the only compliant route — but given your own analysis shows the arbitrage is net-negative/marginal at 1–3 units, the fixed setup and compliance overhead will likely push it into unviability. **Validate unit economics on a small, compliant footprint before committing to a full FDI company.**
- **Do not use a nominee.** It is legally unenforceable and now transparency-exposed.

## 2. Is accommodation open to foreigners, and do you need an IRC?

**Foreign-ownership rule:** Under Vietnam's **WTO Schedule of Specific Commitments in Services** (hotel/lodging, CPC 641), foreign accommodation was originally allowed only "in parallel with investment in the construction, renovation, restoration or acquisition of hotel buildings" for **8 years after accession (i.e., until ~2015)**. **That restriction has expired.** Accommodation/lodging is therefore now effectively **open to 100% foreign ownership** — it is *not* on the foreign-ownership-capped list. (Legal base: **Investment Law 2020, No. 61/2020/QH14**; **Enterprise Law 2020, No. 59/2020/QH14**, as amended by **Law No. 76/2025/QH15**, in force 1 July 2025.)

**"Conditional" — but conditional on operating conditions, not on nationality.** Accommodation is a conditional business line in the sense that you must satisfy operating licenses regardless of ownership:
- Fire prevention & fighting (PCCC) certificate/conditions;
- Security & public-order (ANTT) conditions;
- Tourist-accommodation registration/notification and, for 3-star-plus, class rating, under the **Tourism Law 2017 (No. 09/2017/QH14)**;
- Food-safety certificate if you serve food.
- Guest reporting: every guest (especially foreigners) must be declared to police via the online temporary-residence system.

Penalty for operating an unregistered lodging business: roughly **VND 4–20 million** (~USD 150–770).

**IRC requirement:**
- **Foreign owner → YES, IRC then ERC.** Any foreign investor establishing a company must obtain an **IRC** (Investment Law 2020, Art. 22 & 37) before the **ERC**. (Vietnam's newest 2025/2026 investment amendments move toward allowing the ERC before IRC completion in some cases and are cutting the conditional-business list from 234 to ~196 — but the **exact new statute number/effective date should be confirmed [VERIFY-PRO]**; do not rely on ERC-first for planning yet.)
- **Vietnamese owner → NO IRC.** A 100%-Vietnamese household business or LLC needs only the ERC / household registration.

**STR-specific classification risk (important):** There is genuine ambiguity in how *sub-leasing* is characterized:
- If you provide lodging **with services** (cleaning, reception, linens, etc.) to short-stay guests, it is an **accommodation service** ("dịch vụ lưu trú") under the Tourism Law — the standard, cleanest classification, and the one that gives the better deemed tax rate.
- If it is bare re-letting of a property, it can be read as **property leasing/real estate sub-leasing** under the **Law on Real Estate Business 2023 (No. 29/2023/QH15, in force 1 Jan 2025)**. Foreign-invested entities *can* lease existing property for sub-lease, but that law imposes a narrower scope and extra conditions on foreign-invested companies.
- Also note: several cities (notably **Ho Chi Minh City**) have **banned STR in ordinary residential apartment buildings**, allowing it only in mixed-use/tourism ("condotel") developments. Da Lat's local apartment-management rules must be checked; **villas/standalone homestays carry lower regulatory risk than condo/apartment units.** **[VERIFY-PRO]** the classification (accommodation service vs. real-estate sub-lease) and any Da Lat/Lâm Đồng apartment-building STR restriction.

## 3. Option (a) — Household business (Hộ kinh doanh)

- **Who can own:** **Vietnamese citizens only** (or naturalized citizens). **Foreigners are excluded** — this is the option's dealbreaker for a foreign operator.
- **Liability:** **Unlimited** — the owner is personally liable with all their assets. A concern if a guest is injured or a head-lease dispute escalates.
- **Registration authority (post-July-2025):** the **commune/ward-level** office where the unit sits — the ward People's Committee's **Division of Economic Affairs / Division of Economic Affairs, Infrastructure & Urban Development** (Decree 168/2025/ND-CP). No longer the district office. Under the "non-territorial" policy you may file at any commune office nationwide.
- **Documents:** application form; owner's ID/CCCD; proof of lawful use of the business address (your head-lease must permit the activity); (if a family household) member list.
- **Timeline:** ~**3 working days**.
- **Government fee:** typically **~VND 100,000** (set locally). No IRC, no national-portal publication fee.
- **Tax (from 1 Jan 2026 — major change):** the **lump-sum ("thuế khoán") method is abolished** (per Politburo **Resolution 68-NQ/TW (2025)** and **Resolution 198/2025/QH15**). Household businesses now **declare and pay on actual revenue**:
  - **VAT/PIT exemption** if annual revenue **≤ VND 200 million** (raised from 100M by **VAT Law No. 48/2024/QH15**, effective 1 Jan 2026).
  - Above the threshold, accommodation **service** revenue is taxed at deemed **5% VAT + 2% PIT ≈ 7% of gross revenue** (Circular **40/2021/TT-BTC**, Appendix I, services group). *Bare property leasing* is **5% VAT + 5% PIT ≈ 10%**. **[VERIFY-PRO]** which applies to your operation.
  - **Business license fee (lệ phí môn bài) abolished from 1 Jan 2026** (Resolution 198/2025/QH15).
  - A revised, tiered PIT scheme for larger households (e.g., 17% on profit above certain revenue bands) is **proposed/under development — treat as not-yet-final [VERIFY-PRO].**
- **Scaling:** Weak. One owner, meant for micro-scale; harder to raise capital, add equity partners, or present professionally to landlords/banks. (The old single-location cap has been relaxed, but it remains a small-business form.)
- **Key drawback for thin margins:** deemed **PIT is charged on gross revenue even if you lose money.** Your own numbers show net-negative months — a household would still owe ~2% PIT on gross.

## 4. Option (b) — Single-member LLC (Công ty TNHH MTV)

- **Who can own:** one owner — an individual **or** an organization; can be **100% Vietnamese** (ERC only) **or 100% foreign** (IRC + ERC). This is the natural vehicle for a solo operator who wants a company.
- **Liability:** **Limited** to charter capital — protects personal assets.
- **Tax:** **CIT on profit** under **CIT Law No. 67/2025/QH15** (in force 1 Oct 2025): **15%** if annual revenue ≤ **VND 3 billion**, **17%** if VND 3–50 billion, **20%** above. VAT: normally **10% credit method** (deduct input VAT), or the **5% direct method** if small/eligible. Business license fee abolished from 2026.
- **Big advantage on thin/negative margins:** a company is **taxed on profit, not gross** — you **deduct the head-lease rent, furnishing, utilities, cleaning, platform fees, depreciation**, etc. Given your analysis shows near-zero/negative net, CIT would be near zero, while a household pays ~2% PIT on gross regardless. **For a loss-leading launch phase, the LLC is more income-tax-efficient.** (Counter-point: under the VAT credit method you pay ~10% output VAT with little to credit because residential head-lease rent is VAT-exempt; the household's 5% deemed VAT can be lighter. Net VAT vs. CIT trade-off should be modeled — **[VERIFY-PRO]**.)
- **Registration authority:** **provincial level — the Lâm Đồng Department of Finance (Sở Tài chính)**, which since 1 July 2025 has **absorbed the former Department of Planning & Investment (Sở Kế hoạch & Đầu tư)** and houses the Business Registration Office. For a foreign owner, the IRC is handled by the same provincial investment-registration authority.
- **Documents (domestic):** ERC application, charter, owner ID, address proof. **(Foreign):** additionally the IRC dossier — investment project proposal, financial capacity proof, legalized/notarized passport or parent-company documents, lease/site documents, and a **resident legal representative with a Vietnamese e-ID (VNeID)** (now required for e-filing).
- **Timeline & fees:** see §7.
- **Scaling:** Good. Can sign leases in the company name, issue VAT invoices, add units, later convert to multi-member, borrow, and sell equity.

---

## 5. Option (c) — Multi-member LLC (Công ty TNHH hai thành viên trở lên)

- **Who can own:** **2–50 members** (individuals/organizations, Vietnamese and/or foreign). Limited liability. **This is the right form for a genuine foreign-operator + Vietnamese-partner venture** where both hold *real* equity.
- **Why it matters here:** it lets a foreigner and a Vietnamese partner each hold a **bona-fide equity stake** — the legitimate alternative to a nominee. If any member is foreign, the company is foreign-invested and needs **IRC + ERC**; a Vietnamese-majority structure still triggers IRC once foreign capital is present (percentage affects only certain conditional-sector conditions, which for open accommodation are not restrictive).
- **Governance:** a Members' Council; decisions by capital-weighted vote — draft the charter carefully on deadlock, transfers, profit splits, and manager authority.
- **Tax/authority/timeline:** same regime as the single-member LLC (CIT 15/17/20%; provincial Department of Finance; IRC+ERC if foreign member).
- **Trade-off:** more governance complexity than a single-member LLC, but it is the **clean, enforceable way to combine foreign capital/know-how with a local partner.**

---

## 6. Option (d) — Foreign-owned vs. Vietnamese nominee / local partner

- **100% foreign-owned (LLC via IRC+ERC):** fully legal, fully yours, enforceable — but heaviest cost/time/capital/compliance (resident legal rep, DICA capital account, audited accounts, UBO disclosure). Appropriate only if you are scaling seriously.
- **Nominee (Vietnamese holds the entity/household on paper for a hidden foreign owner): AVOID.**
  - Such side agreements (POAs, declarations of trust, loan-backed control) are treated as **sham/void transactions under the Civil Code**; Vietnamese courts will **not protect** the foreigner's economic interest.
  - The nominee can defect, sell, or refuse to remit profits; the nominee's **spouse** may claim the shares as marital property.
  - **Decree 168/2025/ND-CP mandates UBO disclosure** (25% ownership or control threshold) — hiding the real foreign owner is now a compliance breach that can trigger review/termination, on top of AML exposure.
- **Genuine local partner (real equity in a multi-member LLC): the legitimate middle path.** Give the Vietnamese partner a *real* stake and role, not a paper one. Alternatively, the **Vietnamese partner runs the Vietnamese-owned entity (household or domestic LLC)** and the foreigner contributes under a transparent, arm's-length **management/services agreement** (paid a fee), keeping foreign involvement lawful and above-board. **[VERIFY-PRO]** the structure of any such services agreement.

## 7. Registration steps, authority, timeline, government fees (VND)

**Domestic entity (Vietnamese owner) — household business or domestic LLC:**
- **Household business:** ward-level office; **~3 working days**; fee **~VND 100,000**.
- **Domestic LLC (ERC only):** provincial **Department of Finance / Business Registration Office**; **~3 working days** statutory; ERC fee **VND 50,000** (currently discounted 50% and **VND 0 if filed online** via the National Business Registration Portal through end-2026); mandatory **portal publication fee VND 100,000**. Realistic all-in incl. bank/tax/seal setup: **~VND 3.5–10 million** if done yourself.

**Foreign-owned LLC — IRC → ERC:**
- **Step 1 – IRC:** provincial investment-registration authority (**Department of Finance**), statutory **15 working days**; realistically **3–5 weeks** with clarifications. **No state fee (VND 0)** — costs are translation/legalization/advisory.
- **Step 2 – ERC:** **3 working days**; fees as above (VND 50,000 / VND 0 online + VND 100,000 publication).
- **Step 3 – Capital account (DICA)** and **charter-capital contribution within 90 days of ERC** — missing this risks penalties (~**VND 30–50 million**) and forced capital reduction.
- **Step 4 – Post-licensing:** tax registration, e-invoice, PCCC/ANTT, tourism-accommodation registration.
- **Realistic end-to-end:** **~6–10 weeks** (longer if extra sector consultation is triggered). Legal reference set: Investment Law 2020 (61/2020/QH14); Enterprise Law 2020 (59/2020/QH14) as amended by 76/2025/QH15; **Decree 168/2025/ND-CP** (enterprise registration, replacing **Decree 01/2021/ND-CP**); **Decree 31/2021/ND-CP** (investment implementation, recently amended — **exact amending decree number [VERIFY-PRO]**).
- **Typical real-world FDI setup cost (fees + legalization + advisory):** commonly cited **~VND 26–150 million** (~USD 1,000–5,800).

> **60-day launch reality check:** a domestic household/LLC comfortably fits inside 60 days; a foreign-owned IRC+ERC build is **tight-to-unrealistic** within 60 days once legalization and capital-account steps are counted.

## 8. Charter/registered-capital norms

- **No statutory minimum** for accommodation/general services under the Enterprise/Investment Laws.
- For **foreign-owned** entities the authority applies a **"feasibility/reasonableness" test**: capital must credibly cover your plan. Practitioners commonly register **~USD 10,000 (≈ VND 260 million)** as a workable floor for a small service FDI (lower is sometimes accepted). For 1–3 leased units your upfront needs (deposits + furnishing, per your own data ~USD 7,760–12,500 per unit) already justify roughly this range.
- Sector minimums that do NOT apply to you but are worth knowing: **real-estate *development* VND 20 billion (~USD 769k)** — a reason to keep your activity classified as **accommodation service**, not real-estate development.
- **Domestic household/LLC:** no meaningful capital floor; you self-declare a realistic figure.

## 9. Recommendation for 1–3 units

**Scenario A — You are Vietnamese, or have a genuine trusted Vietnamese co-owner/spouse (recommended lean start):**
1. **Fastest/cheapest:** register a **household business (hộ kinh doanh)** in the Vietnamese person's name at the ward office (~3 days, ~VND 100k). Ideal to *test the model* on 1–3 units within your 60-day window.
2. **If you want limited liability + VAT invoices + a professional face to landlords and clear path to scale:** register a **domestic single-member LLC** (ERC only, no IRC). Given thin/negative margins, the **CIT-on-profit** treatment (15% band, and near-zero when you're not profitable) is *more tax-efficient than the household's ~2% PIT on gross.*
3. Foreigner participates through **real equity in a multi-member LLC** or a **transparent management/services contract** — never a nominee.

**Scenario B — You are a foreigner with no genuine local partner and want to do it properly:**
- The compliant route is a **100% foreign-owned single-member LLC (TNHH MTV)** registering accommodation-service lines, via **IRC + ERC** at the Lâm Đồng Department of Finance, ~USD 10k charter capital, resident legal rep with VNeID.
- **Honest caveat aligned with your own analysis:** at 1–3 units where the arbitrage is net-negative/marginal at asking rents, the **fixed FDI setup + ongoing compliance** (audited accounts, capital account, licenses) will very likely make the venture unviable. **Prove the economics first** (negotiate rents to your max-supportable levels, confirm villa-premium positioning) — ideally via a legitimate local-partner structure — **before** locking in a full FDI company.

**Across all scenarios:**
- Prefer **villas/standalone homestays** over apartment/condo units to avoid residential-building STR bans.
- Ensure every **head-lease explicitly permits sub-leasing and commercial short-stay/lodging use** — this is the foundation of the whole model.
- Budget and file for **PCCC (fire), ANTT (security), and tourism-accommodation registration** under the Tourism Law 2017 regardless of entity type.

# Licenses & Permits to Legally Run Short‑Term Tourist Accommodation (Airbnb / Homestay / Villa) in Đà Lạt, Lâm Đồng

**Prepared:** July 2026 · **FX used:** 26,000 VND/USD · **Scope:** Đà Lạt (now the political‑administrative center of the merged Lâm Đồng province)

> **Read this first — three 2025 structural changes that change the answer vs. older guides:**
> 1. **New Fire law regime.** From **1 July 2025**, the **Law on Fire Prevention, Fighting & Rescue 2024 (Luật 55/2024/QH15)** and **Decree 105/2025/NĐ‑CP** replaced the old **Law 27/2001** and **Decree 136/2020/NĐ‑CP (as amended by Decree 50/2024/NĐ‑CP)**. The thresholds below come from the **new** decree. Older articles you may have seen about Decree 136/2020 Appendix IV/V are no longer the operative text. **[VERIFY‑PRO]** confirm transitional handling of any pre‑July‑2025 approvals.
> 2. **District police abolished (1 March 2025).** The Security & Order (ANTT) certificate and residence functions moved down to **commune/ward police (Công an cấp xã/phường)**. For a small villa/homestay in Đà Lạt the issuing body is now the **ward police**, not a district office.
> 3. **Province merger + two‑tier government (1 July 2025).** Lâm Đồng, Bình Thuận and Đắk Nông merged into a new **Lâm Đồng** province (Resolution **202/2025/QH15**); the district level was removed and Đà Lạt was reorganized into **wards (phường)** sitting directly under the province. Tourism notifications go to the **provincial** Department (Sở Văn hóa, Thể thao và Du lịch tỉnh Lâm Đồng); ward People's Committee/police are your day‑to‑day base authority.

## 0. Foundational prerequisite (not one of your 4, but everything else depends on it)

**Business registration.** Article 49 of the Tourism Law 2017 makes "having business registration" the first condition of running lodging. Options:
- **Hộ kinh doanh (household business)** — normal choice for 1–3 small units. Registered with the **ward/commune** business‑registration desk (the old district Economic Office function moved to commune level). State fee ≈ **100,000 VND**.
- **Doanh nghiệp (company/LLC)** — if scaling; registered with the **provincial Department of Finance** business‑registration office. Online filing is effectively free.
- Register the exact conditional line **"kinh doanh dịch vụ lưu trú"** (accommodation services), and if you'll serve food, **"dịch vụ ăn uống."**

**[VERIFY‑PRO — important for you specifically as a foreign operator]:** A foreign individual generally **cannot** register a *hộ kinh doanh*; and foreign‑invested accommodation/real‑estate‑sublease is a restricted, licensed activity (Investment Law 2020, WTO commitments). You will likely need either a **Vietnamese‑national nominee/partner structure** or an **FDI company with the correct business lines and an Investment Registration Certificate** before any of the four licenses below can be issued in your name. Also confirm the **head‑lease permits subletting and business registration at the address**, and that residential land‑use is acceptable for lodging use. Have a licensed Vietnamese lawyer confirm the ownership structure before signing leases.

## 1. Tourist Accommodation Registration — "cơ sở lưu trú du lịch"
**Legal basis:** Law on Tourism 2017 (**Luật 09/2017/QH14**), Arts. 48–49 & **Art. 29**; **Decree 168/2017/NĐ‑CP** (amended by **Decree 142/2018/NĐ‑CP**).

**What it actually is:** There is **no "operating license"** issued by the tourism authority. Two distinct things exist:

**(a) Mandatory pre‑operation notification (thông báo) — required.**
- **Authority:** Sở Văn hóa, Thể thao và Du lịch tỉnh Lâm Đồng (provincial tourism department).
- **Trigger/timeline:** Under **Art. 29 Decree 168/2017**, at least **15 days before opening**, you must send a written notice stating: (a) name, type, scale of the establishment; (b) address and legal‑representative info; (c) a **commitment that you meet the Art. 49 conditions** (business registration + security/order + fire safety + environment + food safety + minimum physical facilities).
- **Follow‑up:** The department may inspect your **minimum physical‑facility & service conditions ("điều kiện tối thiểu")**. If you fall short, they require you to fix it or reclassify the property type.
- **Cost:** No state fee for the notification.

**Minimum conditions by property type (Decree 168/2017, as amended):**
| Type | Core minimums |
|---|---|
| **Biệt thự du lịch (tourist villa)** | Electricity, clean‑water supply and drainage; distinct reception, bedroom(s), kitchen, bathroom/toilet; bedding & basic furnishings; reception & luggage handling. |
| **Căn hộ du lịch (tourist apartment)** | Lighting, clean water; bed/mattress or mat, blanket, pillow, mosquito net, face & bath towels; kitchen/utensils. |
| **Nhà ở có phòng cho khách thuê / homestay** | Shared living area; guest sleeping area; kitchen; bathroom and toilet; bedding & towels. |

All types must **also** satisfy: business registration, ANTT (§3), fire safety (§2), environmental protection (§4), food safety (if serving food), and a **reception area + signage**.

**(b) Star rating (xếp hạng sao) — VOLUNTARY.**
- Ranking is **optional/tự nguyện** (Art. 50 Tourism Law). 1–3 stars are recognized by the **provincial** department; 4–5 stars by the national tourism authority.
- **Relevant to your strategy:** Your model relies on **premium villa positioning (~$98 ADR)**. A recognized "hạng sao" or officially recognized villa/apartment class can support that positioning, but it is not legally required to operate.
- **Star appraisal fee (Circular 34/2018/TT‑BTC):** 1–2 star = **1,500,000 VND**; 3 star = **2,000,000 VND**; 4–5 star = **3,500,000 VND** per file.

**Documents (voluntary rating):** application form (MCST template); self‑assessment against the national standard (TCVN); staff/manager list; certified copies of the manager's/heads' professional‑training certificates.
**Timeline:** ~30 days for rating recognition; the pre‑operation notification itself is immediate (you just need it on file ≥15 days before opening).

## 2. Fire Prevention & Fighting (PCCC)
**Legal basis (CURRENT):** **Law 55/2024/QH15** (Fire Prevention, Fighting & Rescue, in force 1 July 2025); **Decree 105/2025/NĐ‑CP** (in force 1 July 2025). *(Supersedes Decree 136/2020 + Decree 50/2024.)*

**Key threshold — does your villa need a design appraisal (thẩm định thiết kế PCCC)?**
- Under **Annex III of Decree 105/2025**, "hotels, guesthouses, rest houses and other lodging facilities" need **fire‑design appraisal + fire‑acceptance (nghiệm thu)** only if they are **≥ 7 storeys** OR **≥ 3,000 m² total floor area.**
- **Your units (3BR/2BR/4BR villas & a homestay) are far below both thresholds → NO design appraisal and NO fire‑police acceptance certificate is required.**

**But you are still a "facility under fire‑safety management."**
- Accommodation is on **Annex I of Decree 105/2025** (the list of ~34 categories of facilities under PCCC management), even when below the appraisal thresholds. So instead of a *certificate*, your obligation is **ongoing self‑compliance + being on the ward's fire‑management roster**:
  - Meet the fire‑safety **conditions for a facility** (Law 55/2024): safe electrical system, fire extinguishers, unobstructed **escape route/exit**, warning signage, no illegal gas/electrical loading, a designated person responsible for fire safety, and (for the property class) a basic **internal fire‑safety plan/rules**.
  - **Self‑inspection once per year**, with results reported to the managing authority **before 15 June and 15 December** each year (facilities not in the higher hazard class, Annex II).
  - Subject to **periodic inspection by the ward/commune authority** (the base‑level government now manages small facilities in place of the former district fire police).
- **Practical must‑have:** the **fire‑safety proof document** is what the police ask for inside the **ANTT dossier (§3)**. For a below‑threshold villa this is typically an **on‑site fire‑safety inspection record / fire‑safety commitment (biên bản kiểm tra / cam kết bảo đảm an toàn PCCC)** issued or countersigned by the ward authority — **not** a design‑appraisal certificate. **[VERIFY‑PRO]** confirm with Công an phường in Đà Lạt exactly which fire document they want for a <10‑room villa.

**Cost/timeline:** No state design‑appraisal fee applies (you're below threshold). Real cost is **equipment**: extinguishers, emergency lighting/signage, smoke alarms — budget on the order of **a few million VND per unit** (market cost, not a government fee). Getting the ward inspection/commitment on file: typically days, no statutory fee.

## 3. Security & Order Certificate — "Giấy chứng nhận đủ điều kiện về an ninh, trật tự" (ANTT)
**Legal basis:** **Decree 96/2016/NĐ‑CP** (amended by **Decree 56/2023/NĐ‑CP**). Accommodation ("kinh doanh dịch vụ lưu trú") is listed in Art. 3 as a **conditional business line requiring this certificate** — there is **no small‑scale exemption**; even a 1‑unit villa needs it.

- **Issuing authority (current):** After the abolition of district police, the certificate for small accommodation is issued by the **commune/ward police (Công an phường)** where the unit is located; larger establishments fall to the **provincial police (Phòng Cảnh sát QLHC về TTXH – PC06)**. Your villas/homestay (each well under 10 rooms) → **ward police**. **[VERIFY‑PRO]** confirm the exact ward‑vs‑province cutoff currently applied in Lâm Đồng.
- **Responsible‑person conditions:** 18+, full civil capacity, **no relevant criminal record** (no conviction for national‑security crimes; not under prosecution/preventive measures).
- **Application dossier (Art. 19–23 Decree 96/2016):**
  1. Application form (Mẫu ĐK1 / văn bản đề nghị cấp Giấy chứng nhận);
  2. Certified copy of the **business registration** (with the lodging line);
  3. **Fire‑safety document** — for your below‑threshold villa, the ward fire‑safety inspection record/commitment from §2 (for facilities that require appraisal, the fire‑acceptance certificate instead);
  4. **Background/declaration of the responsible person** (lý lịch / bản khai; judicial‑record confirmation may be requested);
  5. Copy of ID/CCCD of the legal representative;
  6. In practice the ward also expects: **internal security rules, CCTV plan, and lease/premises documents.**
- **Government fee:** **300,000 VND** per issuance (**Circular 218/2016/TT‑BTC**, retained by **Circular 23/2019/TT‑BTC**).
- **Timeline:** **≤ 5 working days** from a complete dossier.
- **Validity:** No fixed expiry; must be **re‑issued on any change** (owner, responsible person, address, business line).
- **Post‑issuance duties (Decree 96/2016 Art. 25 + Residence law):** within **5 days** of starting operation, send the ward police a written notice with a copy of the certificate; maintain a **guest register**; keep **CCTV**; enforce internal security rules; and comply with guest **residence declaration** (see §4d).

## 4. Signboard, Environmental/Hygiene & Ward‑Level Items

**(a) Signboard (biển hiệu).** Law on Advertising 2012 (**Luật 16/2012/QH13**), Art. 34. A compliant **business signboard** (name, address, business line, in the correct size/placement) needs **no separate license**. A **standalone billboard/large advertising structure** would need a construction/advertising notification. Practically: no fee for an ordinary shop‑front sign; just follow the content/size rules. **[VERIFY‑PRO]** if you plan a large illuminated/rooftop sign.

**(b) Environmental registration (đăng ký môi trường).** Law on Environmental Protection 2020 (**Luật 72/2020/QH14**) + **Decree 08/2022/NĐ‑CP**.
- **Likely exempt:** Facilities that generate only **household solid waste < 300 kg/day, wastewater < 5 m³/day, and emissions < 50 m³/hour** treated on‑site are exempt (Art. 32 Decree 08/2022 / Appendix XVI). A small villa/homestay usually falls under this.
- If not exempt, file a simple **environmental registration at the ward People's Committee** (no state fee). **[VERIFY‑PRO]** confirm your specific villa's wastewater volume against the 5 m³/day line, since a full 3–4BR villa at high occupancy can approach it.

**(c) Food safety / hygiene (only if you serve food, e.g., breakfast).** Food Safety Law 2010 (**Luật 55/2010/QH12**) + **Decree 15/2018/NĐ‑CP**.
- If you provide meals/breakfast as a service, you generally need a **"Giấy chứng nhận cơ sở đủ điều kiện an toàn thực phẩm."** Some very small operations are exempt from the certificate but must still file a **food‑safety commitment (bản cam kết ATTP)** and meet hygiene/health‑check conditions.
- **Authority:** base‑level (commune/ward) food‑safety desk for small F&B. State appraisal fee applies. **[VERIFY‑PRO]** the exact fee and whether a certificate vs. commitment applies to your breakfast service.
- **Simplest compliance path:** don't operate a kitchen‑as‑service; offer packaged items or partner with a licensed caterer to avoid the certificate.

**(d) Guest residence declaration (khai báo lưu trú) — operational, mandatory, easy to get fined for.** Law on Residence 2020 (**Luật 68/2020/QH14**), Art. 30.
- For **every guest**, the establishment must report the stay to the residence authority via **VNeID / the National Public Service Portal / ASM software**, **before 23:00** on arrival day (or before **08:00** next morning for late arrivals). Foreign guests must be declared (immigration portal for foreigners).
- **Penalties:** administrative fines for non‑reporting (historically **Decree 144/2021/NĐ‑CP**; note a 2025 penalty decree may now govern — **[VERIFY‑PRO]** the current decree number and amounts). Reported ranges run from ~**500,000–1,000,000 VND** up to **millions of VND** depending on number of unreported guests. This is the single most common day‑to‑day compliance failure for Airbnb operators — build it into check‑in.

## Order of Operations (do them in this sequence)

1. **Confirm ownership/investment structure** (foreign‑ownership + head‑lease sublet rights) with a Vietnamese lawyer. **[VERIFY‑PRO]**
2. **Register the business** (hộ kinh doanh or FDI company) with the lodging line — *everything downstream needs this.*
3. **Fit out the unit to the fire‑safety conditions** (§2) — extinguishers, exits, signage, safe wiring — then obtain the **ward fire‑safety inspection record/commitment.**
4. **Apply for the ANTT certificate** at the ward police (§3) — needs the business registration + fire document. ~5 working days, 300,000 VND.
5. **Handle environmental registration** (§4b) if not exempt; **food‑safety** step (§4c) only if serving meals.
6. **Send the pre‑operation notification** to Sở VH‑TT‑DL Lâm Đồng (§1a) — **at least 15 days before opening.**
7. **(Optional) Apply for star/class rating** (§1b) to support premium‑villa pricing.
8. **Put up a compliant signboard** (§4a).
9. **Go live** — and from night one, run **guest residence declaration** via VNeID/ASM (§4d); keep the guest register and CCTV.
10. **Ongoing:** annual fire self‑inspection + reports (before 15 Jun / 15 Dec); re‑issue ANTT on any change; renew nothing on a fixed calendar for the tourism notice (re‑notify only on change).

# 3. Tax & invoicing

# Da Lat STR Operator — Full Tax & Invoicing Picture (2025–2026)

**Scope:** Short-term-rental subleasing in Da Lat, Lam Dong. FX used: **26,000 VND/USD**. Launch ~Sept 2026, so the **2026 regime is the operative one** (new CIT law live since 1 Oct 2025; lump-sum tax and license fee abolished 1 Jan 2026; new PIT law applies to the 2026 tax year; 8% VAT runs through 31 Dec 2026). Figures below are engineering-grade estimates for planning; anything rate/threshold-sensitive is flagged **[VERIFY-PRO]** for a licensed Vietnamese tax advisor.

> **Key structural change you must design around:** From **1 Jan 2026** the household lump-sum method (*thuế khoán*) is **abolished** (Resolution 68-NQ/TW; Resolution 198/2025/QH15, Art. 10.6). Household businesses now **self-declare** on actual revenue. The old presumptive **percentage-on-revenue rates survive** as the declaration rates, but you can no longer negotiate a flat fixed tax with the ward tax office.

## 1. Headline rates & thresholds (quick table)

| Item | Household business (individual) | Company (LLC) |

| **VAT on accommodation** | 5% presumptive rate, **reduced 20% → 4%** on revenue through 31 Dec 2026 (Decree 174/2025) | Output VAT **8%** (10% standard, cut to 8% through 31 Dec 2026); recover input VAT |
| **Income tax on accommodation** | **PIT 2%** of revenue (percentage method) | **CIT 15%** if revenue ≤3bn; 17% if 3–50bn; else 20% (Law 67/2025) |
| **Tax-free revenue threshold** | **VAT: 200M VND/yr**; **PIT: 500M VND/yr** (2026) **[VERIFY-PRO]** — see §2 | n/a (companies taxed from VND 1) |
| **Business license fee (môn bài)** | **0 — abolished 1 Jan 2026** (Decree 362/2025) | **0 — abolished 1 Jan 2026** |
| **E-invoice** | Mandatory under declaration method; **cash-register e-invoice if revenue ≥1bn/yr** (accommodation is a listed sector) | Mandatory for all sales |

## 2. VAT — 8% vs 10%, and the household 4%

- **Accommodation services are eligible for the reduced 8% rate.** The 2% cut (Resolution 204/2025/QH15; **Decree 174/2025/ND-CP**, in force 1 Jul 2025 – **31 Dec 2026**) explicitly covers accommodation and food-service sectors. Excluded sectors (finance, banking, securities, insurance, real-estate business, telecoms, metals, mining ex-coal, special-consumption-tax goods) do **not** include lodging. So a **company** charges guests **8% VAT** on the room rate; from **1 Jan 2027 it reverts to 10%** unless extended.
- **Household/percentage method:** accommodation carries a **5% VAT presumptive rate** (Circular 40/2021/TT-BTC, Appendix I — "dịch vụ lưu trú"). Decree 174/2025 gives percentage-method taxpayers a **20% cut of that rate** for eligible services, i.e. **5% → 4%** on invoices issued through 31 Dec 2026. **[VERIFY-PRO]** that your ward tax office applies the 20% reduction to the lodging percentage rate (confirmed in the decree text, but confirm the invoice mechanics).
- **Input VAT trap for a company:** your biggest cost — **rent paid to an individual landlord — carries no deductible VAT invoice** (the landlord is on the presumptive regime). So the 8% you charge guests is largely un-offset by input credit on rent; only utilities/supplies/OTA fees with proper VAT invoices are recoverable.

## 3. Household "thuế khoán" → declaration: the % split

For **accommodation services** (short-term lodging for tourists — the STR case), Circular 40/2021 Appendix I sets:

- **VAT 5% + PIT 2% = 7%** of gross revenue (before the 2026 VAT reduction; ~**6% effective** once VAT drops to 4%).

Do **not** confuse this with **bare property leasing** (*cho thuê tài sản*), which is **VAT 5% + PIT 5% = 10%** — that higher rate is what your **landlord** pays, not what you pay for running lodging (see §6).

**2026 exemption thresholds (flag):**
- **VAT exemption: revenue ≤ 200M VND/yr** (Law on VAT 48/2024/QH15, effective 1 Jan 2026).
- **PIT exemption: revenue ≤ 500M VND/yr** (Law on PIT **109/2025/QH15**, passed 10 Dec 2025; effective 1 Jul 2026, **applies to business income for the 2026 tax year**).
- **[VERIFY-PRO]:** the VAT (200M) and PIT (500M) thresholds are on different figures, creating a **200M–500M band where VAT is due but PIT is not**. Also confirm whether 2026 treats the threshold as a **deductible allowance** ((Revenue − threshold) × rate) rather than the old Circular-40 **cliff** (any revenue above threshold made the *whole* amount taxable). Sources indicate a shift to the deduction approach, but the implementing decree mechanics should be confirmed.
- **New for 500M–3bn:** under Law 109/2025 an individual may **elect 15% on net income** instead of the percentage-of-revenue method — useful if your margins are thin. **[VERIFY-PRO]** the election mechanics and required bookkeeping.

## 4. CIT (company) and PIT

- **CIT (Law 67/2025/QH15, effective 1 Oct 2025; Decree 320/2025):** standard **20%**; **15%** if total annual revenue **≤ 3bn VND**; **17%** if **3–50bn**. A 3–4 unit Da Lat portfolio (~1.3–1.5bn/yr) sits at **15%**. A company **newly converted from a household business** gets a **2-year CIT exemption** — relevant if you start as a household and incorporate later. Losses carry forward up to 5 years.
- **PIT:** the percentage-of-revenue rates above are the operator's PIT. If instead you *employ* staff, you withhold employee PIT on the progressive scale (new law cuts 7 brackets to 5, top rate 35%, effective 2026 tax year).

## 5. Business license fee (*lệ phí môn bài*)

**Abolished from 1 Jan 2026** for all enterprises, organisations, households and individuals — Resolution 198/2025/QH15 and **Decree 362/2025/ND-CP** (31 Dec 2025) repealing Decree 139/2016 and Decree 22/2020. **Budget 0 VND.**

For reference, the **pre-2026 tiers** (no longer charged) were:
- *Households/individuals:* revenue >500M → 1,000,000/yr; >300–500M → 500,000; >100–300M → 300,000; ≤100M → exempt.
- *Companies:* charter capital >10bn → 3,000,000/yr; ≤10bn → 2,000,000; branch/location → 1,000,000. First year of establishment was exempt.

## 6. Withholding when you PAY the landlord (master lease)

The landlord is an **individual leasing out property** = bare-lease category: **VAT 5% + PIT 5% = 10%** of rent (Circular 40/2021), due only if the landlord's **total rental revenue > 200M/yr (VAT) / 500M/yr (PIT)**.

- **Who declares depends on your structure:**
  - **Company operator:** the lease can name the **company as the paying/withholding agent** — it declares and remits the landlord's 5%+5% on the landlord's behalf (Circular 40/2021, Art. 8). Standard practice; put it in the contract and gross-up or net accordingly.
  - **Household/individual operator:** an individual **cannot** be a statutory withholding agent — the **landlord self-declares** at the ward tax office. You can still shift the economic burden by contract, but the filing duty is the landlord's.
- **[VERIFY-PRO]:** whether the 20% VAT reduction applies to property leasing — bare leasing is arguably "real-estate business," which is **excluded** from the 8%/reduction, so assume the landlord's VAT stays at **5%** (no cut).

**Worked (context rents, annual):**
- Xuan Huong villa **$600/mo = 187,200,000 VND/yr** → **below 200M** → landlord exempt, **withholding 0**.
- Hillside villa **$850/mo = 265,200,000 VND/yr** → above 200M → **VAT 13,260,000 + PIT 13,260,000 = 26,520,000 VND/yr (~$1,020)** payable on that lease. Structure who bears it before you sign.

## 7. Foreign Contractor Tax / Airbnb

Two distinct flows:

**(a) Airbnb withholds YOUR (host) presumptive tax at source.** Per Airbnb's Vietnam guidance, it withholds from payouts:
- Resident **individual/household** host: **5% VAT + 2% PIT = 7%**.
- **Non-resident** individual: **5% VAT + 5% PIT = 10%**.
- **Company** with a valid **10-digit Enterprise Registration Certificate: 0%** (you self-declare).
→ Practical point: a **household** host on Airbnb effectively has ~7% skimmed and remitted for it; **reconcile this against your own VAT/PIT filing to avoid double-paying** on Airbnb-sourced revenue. A **company** avoids the skim but must invoice and declare its own 8% VAT + CIT. **[VERIFY-PRO]** how the tax office credits Airbnb-withheld amounts against your declaration.

**(b) FCT on Airbnb's own commission (the service fee Airbnb charges you).** A cross-border service by a foreign contractor: **VAT 5% + CIT 5% = 10%** of the commission (Circular 103/2014; foreign-supplier rules in Circular 80/2021 and the 2025 VAT law).
- If Airbnb **registers** on the GDT foreign-supplier e-portal, it self-pays and you don't withhold. **As of latest reporting, Airbnb, Booking.com, Agoda and PayPal had *not* registered**, and GDT directed ~100 banks/payment intermediaries to withhold on their behalf. **[VERIFY-PRO] — enforcement is in flux.** A **company** paying commission directly to an unregistered foreign contractor may itself have to **self-withhold FCT**; a household typically won't be pursued for this.
- Worked (villa, assuming a 15% Airbnb host-only fee): commission ≈ **73,008,000 VND/yr**; FCT 10% ≈ **7,300,800 VND/yr** — usually absorbed at platform/bank level, not yours, but confirm.

## 8. E-invoices (Decree 123/2020, as amended by Decree 70/2025)

- **Companies:** must issue **registered e-invoices** for every booking; VAT shown separately.
- **Household under declaration method (2026):** must issue **e-invoices**. If **annual revenue ≥ 1bn VND** and you're in a listed B2C sector — **accommodation/hotel and F&B are named** — you must use **e-invoices generated from a cash register connected in real time to the tax authority** (Decree 70/2025/ND-CP, effective **1 Jun 2025**). A 3-unit Da Lat portfolio will exceed 1bn, so **budget for cash-register e-invoicing software integrated with GDT**.
- **Revenue ≤ 200M/yr (2026):** exempt from tax **and** from the e-invoice requirement.

## 9. Worked micro-example — Xuan Huong Lake House 3BR villa

**Assumptions:** ADR $78 = **2,028,000 VND**; **20 booked nights/mo**; master rent $600/mo. Gross room revenue = **40,560,000 VND/mo = 486,720,000 VND/yr (~$18,720)**. (Context net margin ≈ **−$50/mo at asking rent** → roughly break-even/slightly loss-making pre-tax, so income tax is near-zero this year but **VAT and withholding still bite**.)

**A) As a HOUSEHOLD business (single unit, 2026 declaration method)**
- VAT: 5% → **4%** (Decree 174/2025) × 486,720,000 = **19,468,800 VND/yr (~$749)**.
- PIT: revenue **486.72M < 500M** → **PIT 0** (if this is the person's only business). *A 3-unit household aggregating >500M pays 2% PIT on full revenue.*
- License fee: **0**. FCT/commission: platform-level.
- **Household total ≈ 19.5M VND/yr (~$749)**, VAT-only — but note Airbnb would withhold 7% (≈34M) at source if all bookings ran through it; reconcile so you don't overpay.

**B) As a COMPANY (LLC, deduction method)**
- Output VAT **8%** × 486,720,000 = **38,937,600 VND** collected from guests, remitted **net of recoverable input VAT** (rent gives no input credit; utilities/supplies/OTA fees do). VAT is guest-borne, not a P&L cost.
- CIT: at ~break-even, **≈ 0** this year (losses carry forward 5 yrs); if profitable and revenue ≤3bn, **15%** (e.g. 50M profit → **7,500,000 VND**).
- License fee: **0**. Airbnb withholds **0** (10-digit ERC) — you self-declare.

**C) Master-lease withholding on this unit:** rent 187.2M/yr **< 200M → landlord exempt, 0 withholding.**

**Read-through:** the household route is cheaper and simpler at ~500M/unit revenue (VAT-only, PIT-exempt, no CIT, ~4% effective tax on turnover). The **company** route wins once you scale (multiple units pushing aggregate well over 500M where household PIT kicks in at 2% of *all* turnover regardless of profit), because CIT is **15% of profit, not revenue** — and profit is thin/negative here, so a company can shelter losses while a percentage-method household pays on gross. Model both against your actual negotiated rents.

## PART 1 — COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST

### A. Guest registration — this is YOUR job, not the guest's

Legally, "người trực tiếp quản lý, điều hành cơ sở lưu trú" (the operator/host) must register every guest. As the sublease operator you ARE that person. Do not assume guests self-register.

**A1. Vietnamese guests — "Thông báo lưu trú" (lodging notification)**
Legal basis: **Article 30, Law on Residence No. 68/2020/QH14**; penalties under **Decree 282/2025, Article 10**.

- [ ] Notify local police (công an cấp xã/phường where the unit sits) of **every** overnight guest.
- [ ] **Deadline: before 23:00 on the day the stay begins.** If the guest checks in after 23:00, notify **before 08:00 the next morning.**
- [ ] **Data to collect & submit:** full name; personal ID number (số định danh cá nhân / CCCD 12-digit) **or** passport number; reason for stay; duration of stay; address of stay.
- [ ] **How:** the **VNeID** app (Thủ tục hành chính → Thông báo lưu trú), the **National Public Service Portal / Bộ Công an public-service portal**, or the new unified portal **tbltkbtt.bocongan.gov.vn** (launched 2026, consolidating Vietnamese lodging + foreigner declaration). In-person, phone, or email to the ward police also legally allowed.
- [ ] Keep a **guest log (sổ/registry)** with check-in/out and ID data.

**A2. Foreign guests — "Khai báo tạm trú cho người nước ngoài" (temporary-residence declaration)**
Legal basis: **Article 33, Law on Entry, Exit, Transit & Residence of Foreigners No. 47/2014/QH13** (amended by 51/2019/QH14 and 23/2023/QH15); penalties under **Decree 282/2025** (entry-exit/residence chapter replacing old Decree 144/2021 Art. 18).

- [ ] Declare **within 12 hours** of the foreigner arriving at the property (Da Lat is urban → 12h; the 24h window is only for remote/mountainous areas). **[VERIFY-PRO]** confirm your ward's classification, but assume 12h.
- [ ] **Data:** passport/travel-document number, full name, nationality, date of birth, visa/temporary-residence-card details, entry date, intended stay, accommodation address.
- [ ] **How:** the **provincial Immigration online declaration portal** (Phòng Quản lý Xuất nhập cảnh, Công an tỉnh Lâm Đồng), the Bộ Công an public-service portal, or the unified **tbltkbtt.bocongan.gov.vn**. **Note:** the old standalone "ASM" temporary-residence software / legacy site was **discontinued (from 21 May 2026)** — do not use it.
- [ ] Declaration is **free**. Register your operator account with the provincial immigration office **before** your first foreign check-in (account approval takes a few days).
- [ ] Check the guest's visa/entry stamp is valid; you can be penalized for harboring an over-stayer.

**A3. Housekeeping**
- [ ] Photograph/scan each guest's CCCD/passport at check-in (see Part 3 for how to store legally).
- [ ] Do the declaration the same day — set it as a check-in SOP for every unit.

### B. Fire safety (PCCC) — operational compliance

Legal basis: **Law 55/2024/QH15**; **Decree 105/2025/NĐ-CP** (technical requirements); penalties under **Decree 106/2025/NĐ-CP**. A rented villa/homestay run as accommodation is a facility "with lodging function" on the PCCC-management list, and a **house-combined-with-business of ≥50 m² business area is expressly a PCCC-managed facility** (Decree 105/2025, Appendix I).

- [ ] **Fire extinguishers:** ABC dry-powder and/or CO₂ portable extinguishers — at least **1-2 per floor** and near the kitchen; in-date, pressure-checked; inspect every ~6 months.
- [ ] **Escape routes:** at least **two ways out per floor** (main door + secondary door or escape window); keep them **unlocked and unobstructed at all times** (blocked/locked exits are the single most expensive fire violation — see table).
- [ ] **Fire/smoke detection:** smoke detectors (independent battery-backup units acceptable for a small villa) in bedrooms, hallways, and near the kitchen.
- [ ] **Emergency lighting + EXIT signs:** auto-on-battery emergency lights on stairs/corridors; illuminated "EXIT / LỐI THOÁT" directional signs.
- [ ] **Escape-route floor plan (sơ đồ thoát nạn)** posted on each floor / in each room.
- [ ] **Internal fire-safety rules (nội quy PCCC)** and prohibition/warning/instruction signs (biển cấm, biển báo, biển chỉ dẫn) posted in visible spots — **legally required and separately fined if missing.**
- [ ] **Escape hoods / anti-smoke masks (mặt nạ lọc độc):** required where the building is **≥3 floors** — roughly 1 per person present (guests + staff), stocked on each floor. Many Da Lat villas are 3-4 storeys, so budget for these.
- [ ] **Basic breaking tools** (búa, kìm cộng lực) kit.
- [ ] **Records/dossier (hồ sơ quản lý PCCC)** and periodic **self-inspection**; keep receipts and inspection logs.
- [ ] **Compulsory fire/explosion insurance (bảo hiểm cháy nổ bắt buộc)** under **Decree 67/2023/NĐ-CP** — mandatory for accommodation facilities on the fire-risk list; premium ≈ **0.05%** of asset value with an automatic suppression system or **0.1%** without. **[VERIFY-PRO]** whether each specific villa falls on the mandatory list given its floor count/area — if it does, non-purchase is fined under Decree 106/2025.
- [ ] **[VERIFY-PRO]** whether any unit needs a formal PCCC design-appraisal/acceptance (thẩm duyệt/nghiệm thu) or only self-declared safety conditions — depends on scale/height under Decree 105/2025 Appendices; a small ≤ certain-scale homestay usually only needs "fire-safety conditions," not full appraisal, but confirm per building.

### C. Data privacy of guest IDs

Legal basis: **Law on Personal Data Protection No. 91/2025/QH15** (effective 1 Jan 2026) + **Decree 13/2023/NĐ-CP**.

- [ ] **Consent + purpose limitation:** collect ID data only for the lawful purposes (police registration, booking contract) and tell guests why. A short privacy notice at check-in covers this.
- [ ] **Data minimization:** capture only what registration needs; don't over-photograph.
- [ ] **Security:** store scans encrypted / access-controlled (password-protected cloud folder, not a public Google Drive link or a shared WhatsApp group); restrict staff access.
- [ ] **No sale/sharing:** selling or trading personal data is **explicitly prohibited** under Law 91/2025 — never pass guest IDs to third parties beyond the authorities.
- [ ] **Retention limit:** delete ID scans once the legal/registration purpose ends; don't keep passports indefinitely "for records."
- [ ] **Breach handling:** have a basic procedure to notify authorities/affected guests if IDs leak.
- [ ] **[VERIFY-PRO]** whether your processing volume triggers a formal Data-Protection Impact Assessment dossier / MPS notification under Law 91/2025 & Decree 13/2023 — small operators may qualify for lighter obligations, but the sanctioning decree for PDP is still being finalized and fines can be severe.

## PART 3 — PRACTICAL FIRE-SAFETY KIT (small villa/homestay)

Minimum shopping/setup list per building (3-4 storey Da Lat villa):
- **Extinguishers:** 1-2 ABC powder + optionally 1 CO₂ near kitchen/electrical, **per floor**; log 6-monthly checks.
- **Smoke detectors:** each bedroom + hallway + kitchen area (battery-backup independent units OK for small villas).
- **Emergency lighting** on stairs/corridors + **illuminated EXIT/LỐI THOÁT signs** at each exit.
- **Escape hoods (mặt nạ lọc độc):** stock ~1 per occupant on each floor if building is **≥3 floors**.
- **Basic breaking tools** (hammer, bolt cutter) kit near an exit.
- **Posted on each floor:** escape-route floor plan (sơ đồ thoát nạn), internal fire rules (nội quy PCCC), warning/prohibition/instruction signs.
- **Two independent exits per floor**, always unlocked and clear.
- **Records folder:** equipment receipts, inspection log, self-check reports, insurance certificate.
- Confirm each unit's **two escape paths** during your pre-lease inspection — reject/renegotiate any villa where the only second exit is a barred window, since blocked-exit fines (30-50M VND) dwarf a month's margin.
