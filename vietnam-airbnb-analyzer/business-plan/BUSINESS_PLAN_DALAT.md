# Da Lat Short-Term-Rental (Airbnb) Sublease Business — Business Plan

_Compiled July 2026 · USD basis, FX 26,000 VND/USD · Start market: **Da Lat, Lâm Đồng, Vietnam**_

> ⚠️ **Not legal, tax, or investment advice.** Figures marked **[sourced]** come from the AirROI licensed Airbnb API and public rental portals; operating costs are **[estimated]**. Legal/tax content is summarized from the research in `COMPANY_SETUP_VIETNAM.md` and must be confirmed with a licensed Vietnamese lawyer/accountant. Vietnamese law changed a lot in 2025–2026.

---

## 1. Executive summary

We lease houses/villas long-term from owners in Da Lat and sublet them nightly on Airbnb and domestic OTAs. The model is **rental arbitrage**: our margin is the spread between the long lease we pay and the nightly revenue we earn, minus operating costs.

**The honest headline:** Da Lat is the *hardest* of Vietnam's STR markets — lowest occupancy (market avg ~39%) and modest ADR. **At asking rents it loses money.** It becomes profitable only with three disciplines executed together:
1. **Negotiate rent ~15–20% below asking** (to each unit's break-even "max rent").
2. **Position as a premium pool/lake villa** and hold a nightly rate near the top-operator level (~$100/night for a 3BR villa vs the $60 market median).
3. **Price aggressively for weekends, Tết, and the cool-season holidays** — Da Lat demand is domestic and spiky; peak months carry the year.

Executed that way, a lead 3BR lake villa nets **~$390/month (24% margin)**, and a **2-unit launch portfolio nets ~$518/month (~$6,200/year, ~36% cash-on-cash on ~$17.4k deployed)**. Miss any of the three levers and it goes negative.

**Recommended launch:** **1–2 villas/houses** (not apartments), lean legal footprint, live within **60 days**.

## 2. Why Da Lat, and the honest reality

**Pros:** cool climate + lake/pine scenery drive year-round *domestic* tourism (Tết, weekends, school holidays); villa/homestay stock (not condos), which carries **lower STR legal risk** than apartments (Vietnam cities like HCMC restrict STR in residential apartment buildings; standalone houses/villas are cleaner). Low labour cost (cleaning ~$3/hr via bTaskee, which has a Da Lat office).

**Cons (be clear-eyed):** low weekday occupancy; ADR ceiling; weather (rainy May–Oct); the whole model depends on weekend/holiday spikes and premium positioning. This is an *operations* business, not a passive one.

**Sourced market data (134 real Da Lat listings, AirROI):**
| Segment | Median occ | Median ADR | Top-operator ADR @ occ | Top-operator revenue/yr |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Villa/house | 24% | $60 | **$98 @ 50%** | **$15,610** |
| Apartment/homestay | 27% | $39 | $56 @ 53% | $9,128 |

Takeaway: **villas win** in Da Lat, and the money is in *high nightly rate at ~50% occupancy*, not full calendars.

## 3. Product & strategy

- **Target unit:** 3–4BR **house/villa** near Xuân Hương Lake / central Da Lat or a scenic hillside, with (or able to add) a garden/BBQ/fireplace — what domestic groups book for weekends.
- **Guest:** Vietnamese families & friend-groups (weekends/holidays) + some foreign leisure. Design for **groups** (sleeps 6–10), not couples.
- **Distribution:** Airbnb + **Booking.com, Agoda, Traveloka** + local **Facebook/Zalo homestay groups** (critical for domestic demand).
- **Pricing:** dynamic, weekend/holiday premium (target weekend ≈ 1.6–2.0× weekday; Tết & cool-season holidays much higher). See `OPERATIONS_PLAYBOOK.md`.
- **Positioning:** professional photos, fast Wi-Fi, self-check-in smart lock, spotless turnovers, 5-star review flywheel.

### 4.4 Seasonality & break-even
Da Lat revenue is front/back-loaded (Tết Jan–Feb, cool-season & weekend peaks; rainy May–Oct trough). Underwrite to the **annual** figure, keep a cash buffer for the low season, and don't over-commit rent you can't cover in June–September. Portfolio **break-even occupancy ≈ 55%** at the negotiated rents; below that, cut rent or ADR isn't high enough.

## 5. The #1 risk lever: rent negotiation
Because margins live or die on rent, every lease must land at or below its **"max supportable rent"** (in the dashboard leaderboard). For the lead villa that's **$550** (asking $600) — target **$500** on a 2–3 year lease in exchange for stability and upfront furnishing. A longer lease also lets you amortize furnishing over 36 months, which materially lifts net.

## 6. Legal structure & compliance (summary — full detail in `COMPANY_SETUP_VIETNAM.md`)

- **Entity (decisive on who owns it):**
  - **Household business (hộ kinh doanh)** — cheapest/fastest (~3 days, ~VND 100k) but **Vietnamese citizens only**; unlimited liability. From **1 Jan 2026** the lump-sum tax is abolished — you declare on actual revenue; **VAT/PIT exempt under VND 200M/yr (~$7.7k) revenue**, then accommodation service ≈ **7% of revenue (5% VAT + 2% PIT)**; the business-license fee is **abolished** in 2026.
  - **Domestic single-member LLC** — limited liability, VAT invoices, room to scale; no IRC if 100% Vietnamese-owned.
  - **Foreigner** — **cannot** use a household business and **must not** use a nominee (now exposed by UBO rules under Decree 168/2025). The clean route is a **100% foreign-owned LLC with IRC + ERC** — more cost/paperwork; validate economics before committing.
- **Licenses (order of operations):** ① tourist-accommodation registration (Tourism Law 2017) → ② **fire prevention (PCCC)** certificate/commitment (Decree 136/2020 + 50/2024) → ③ **security & order (ANTT)** police certificate (Decree 96/2016). Standalone villas are simpler than large properties.
- **Guest compliance (ongoing):** every guest — Vietnamese and foreign — must be **registered with local police online, typically within 24h** of check-in; non-compliance fines under **Decree 144/2021**. Build ID capture into check-in.
- **Note on the 2025 reform:** Lâm Đồng province was enlarged and the district tier abolished (two-tier province + ward). File at the **ward** office covering the unit. **[VERIFY-PRO]** the exact ward.

## 7. Getting properties from owners (see `contracts/`)
We sign a **master lease** that explicitly grants the right to **sublet short-term**, furnish, register the business + guests at the address, and cooperate on licensing. Vietnamese Civil Code requires the **landlord's consent to sublease** — silence isn't enough, so where the main lease is silent we attach the **Landlord Consent Addendum**. Guests accept the **Guest Terms & House Rules** (including ID for mandatory police registration). All three templates are in `contracts/` — have a Vietnamese lawyer review and notarize.

## 8. 60-day launch plan (from company formation to first bookings)

**Weeks 1–2 — Entity & scouting**
- Decide entity (household biz if Vietnamese/partner; else LLC+IRC). File registration; get tax code + e-invoice setup.
- Shortlist 4–6 candidate villas/houses (lake/central/hillside, sleeps 6–10). Verify each is legally usable for accommodation and check the ward's rules.
- Open a business bank account; line up ~$20–24k capital.

**Weeks 3–4 — Secure units & licenses**
- Negotiate 2 master leases at/below "max rent" (target $500 villa, $400 homestay), 2–3 yr term, using the `contracts/` templates + landlord consent. Sign & (where needed) notarize.
- Start the **PCCC** (fire) and **ANTT** (police security/order) applications and the **tourist-accommodation registration**. Buy extinguishers, alarms, exit signage.

**Weeks 5–6 — Furnish & build the listing**
- Furnish for groups (bedding, kitchen, BBQ/fireplace, fast Wi-Fi, smart lock). Deep clean.
- **Professional photography** (single biggest ADR lever). Write listings (EN + VN). Set up PMS/channel manager + PriceLabs dynamic pricing with weekend/holiday premiums.
- Onboard **bTaskee** (Da Lat) for turnovers; set up laundry, welcome kit, guest-comms templates, and the **police guest-registration** workflow.

**Weeks 7–8 — List, launch, optimize**
- Publish on **Airbnb + Booking.com + Agoda + Traveloka** and local **Facebook/Zalo** groups. Introductory pricing + push for first 5-star reviews.
- Take first bookings; run turnovers; register every guest with police within 24h.
- Review pricing weekly; lock in the coming weekend/holiday premiums.

**Exit-of-60-days target:** 2 units live and legally compliant, first reviews in, on track to the ~$518/mo portfolio net as the review flywheel and weekend/holiday pricing ramp.

## 9. Risks & mitigations
| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Occupancy below plan** (Da Lat weekday softness) | Underwrite to 55% break-even; negotiate low rent; lean on weekend/holiday premium; multi-OTA + domestic FB/Zalo |
| **Rent too high** | Never sign above "max rent"; longer lease for lower rate + 36-mo amortization |
| **Legal/tax missteps** | Lawyer-reviewed contracts; correct entity; do licenses in order; register guests every time; keep e-invoices |
| **Foreign-ownership overhead** | If foreign, validate 1-unit economics before the full LLC+IRC; consider a genuine Vietnamese partner |
| **Seasonal cash crunch (rainy months)** | 3-month buffer; don't over-lease; monthly/mid-term stays in the trough |
| **Furnishing capex sunk if unit underperforms** | Start with 1 villa, prove it, then add the 2nd |
